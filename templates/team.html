Around the world, less than 1% of students will have access to a meaningful cultural exchange experience as part of their secondary education. Among that 1%, less than ⅓ will be students of color.

How can we raise a generation of diverse, creative, globally-minded problem solvers, if experiences like study abroad are limited to so few?

Many of us at Project Exchange have experienced barriers to study abroad ourselves, whether they be due to financial reasons, lack of documentation, family commitments, the fact that we didn’t even know “study abroad” was an option, or something else entirely. Some of us have been lucky to receive support to study abroad, especially through the U.S. Department of State exchange programs like NSLI-Y, YES Abroad, and Youth Ambassadors.

As one example, before serving as an U.S. Youth Ambassador to Uruguay in 2018, our founder Ashley didn't think she’d be able to study abroad. Upon returning home, Ashley was determined to help others access the transformative learning experience she had. And although in-person exchanges weren't scalable, Ashley thought back to how she got to know her host family over WhatsApp, even before she arrived in Uruguay. What if we could replicate that aspect of the experience?

Ashley quickly created a Google Form, and sent it to friends in the U.S. and Uruguay. She said, “fill this out to be matched with a new friend abroad on WhatsApp!” Within a couple hours, there were dozens of responses. Upon seeing the immense interest in digital-first cultural exchange, Ashley started gathering a team, designing curriculum, and building the groundwork for what became Project Exchange: a youth-led, 501c3 nonprofit increasing access to cross-cultural education.
